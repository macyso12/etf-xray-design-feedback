Quick wins
Quick win
Add dollar value input (not just share count)
Users want to enter how much they've invested in $ rather than number of shares. Also fixes mutual fund vs ETF symbol mismatch — if someone has VFIAX they can just enter the dollar amount instead of converting to VOO shares.
"Allowing me to input a price in USD would make the problem go away."
3 upvotes
Quick win
Make colors encode meaning, not decoration
Multiple users confused about what the green shading means. Currently it's purely aesthetic. Should map to sector, market cap, or weight — with a clear legend. This is low-hanging fruit that makes the treemap immediately more useful.
"What do the colors represent? If it's darker green does it mean something?" / "The colors don't indicate anything yet. I just thought it looks pretty."

Quick win
Add individual stock tickers alongside ETFs
Users who hold individual stocks (e.g. AAPL, NVDA directly) want to include them in the analysis so they can see total concentration across ETFs + individual positions combined.
"Allowing stock tickers also so you can see your concentration including individual stocks you own."

Medium
Expand European & international ETF support
A significant portion of commenters couldn't use the tool at all because their ETFs weren't supported. Mentioned tickers: VWCE, VWRA, VWRP, VUAG, MEUD, HCHS, VFEG, XMWX, INRG, Schwab ETFs. European investors are a big underserved segment.
"None of my ETFs are available." / "It doesn't appear to have VWCE which I thought was pretty fundamental."

Medium
Support bonds, crypto, and mutual funds
OP themselves flagged this. Users with bond ETFs, crypto, and Fidelity/Vanguard mutual funds want a complete portfolio picture, not just equity ETFs. The tool loses a lot of value if significant parts of someone's portfolio are excluded.
"I also own some fidelity mutual funds, bond ETF and crypto — that's what I am planning to add as next steps."
8 upvotes
Medium
Switch to SEC EDGAR / NPORT data source
A commenter with experience flagged that scraping iShares/Vanguard/Blackrock directly is fragile. The better approach is pulling from SEC's EDGAR NPORT filings using the CIK number — quarterly, structured, and authoritative. More reliable long-term.
"The best way is to log the cik number and go out to SEC's Edgar data and download the nport."
2 upvotes
Medium
401k + brokerage combined view
Users want to combine holdings across accounts — 401k, brokerage, IRA — into a single exposure view. This is what separates a toy from a real financial tool. Would likely require some kind of portfolio saving/session feature.
"Would be great to see this for a full portfolio across 401k and brokerage together."

Big features
Big feature
Overlap / duplication detection
The core "aha moment" of the tool — showing users they're buying the same companies 5 different ways. Should be a dedicated view: "You're buying MSFT through 4 ETFs." This is the top comment's reaction and the main value prop.
"My 'diversified' portfolio is just 40% Apple and Microsoft with extra steps."
41 upvotes
Big feature
Shareable / exportable report
The top-voted aesthetic comment ("would hang this on my wall") signals demand for a shareable output — PNG export, shareable link, or PDF. Useful for posting to r/personalfinance, sharing with a financial advisor, or just saving for reference.
"I just like it for the color scheme. Would hang this on my wall."
1 upvote
Big feature
Reverse lookup: "which ETFs hold this stock?"
One commenter mentioned difficulty reducing NVDA exposure — this implies a reverse lookup feature: enter a stock ticker and see all the ETFs in your portfolio (or universally) that hold it, and at what weight. Useful for managing concentration actively.
"I spent a ton of time trying to reduce my nvidia exposure, and it was HARD."

UX issues to fix
UX issue
It's not clear what ETF is being shown
First-time users don't understand the input — one commenter couldn't figure out which ETF the visualization was for. The input section and the output need to be more clearly connected. Show the active ETFs at the top of the results.
"I can't tell what ETF this is for. Am I missing something?"
3 upvotes
UX issue
Small cells have no label or number
Treemap cells below a certain size show neither a ticker nor a percentage, leaving users guessing. Should either show a tooltip on hover, display "< 0.1%", or consolidate into a labeled "Other" group rather than silently disappearing.
"The ones with no number — are they supposed to be 0.1% or less? What if there's no name nor no number?"
2 upvotes
UX issue
Vertical bars suggested over horizontal
The horizontal bar chart layout for sector/geography breakdown drew criticism. Vertical bars would feel more natural for comparison. Alternatively, a donut or proportional area chart could work better for composition data specifically.
"Personally I'd have the bar graphs being vertical instead of horizontal. This layout hurts."
